Cloud computing at its core is the use of traditional computing services via the internet, which is what we refer to as ‘the cloud’. Microsoft Azure states that the clouds potential means it can deliver services including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence over the cloud to offering faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scales (What Is Cloud Computing? A Beginner’s Guide | Microsoft Azure, n.d.). Effectively, cloud computing gives the ability for users to access, store and modify data on a device that is saved back to a data center for access when required.

There are key advantages that the cloud service has over an traditional computing service, such as the scalability, storage, data security, data loss protection, and ongoing maintenance (Simplilearn, 2020):

With the rise and innovation of cloud computing, so to comes the broadening of its scope. Today, cloud computing not only refers to the process, but the infrastructure technology that is used to make the cloud work. Further, infrastructure can be adapted and utilized in arrangements of both physical and virtual technology, depending on the service requirements for the cloud to work.

Virtualized infrastructure may include services, operating system software, networks, and infrastructure that can abstract, pool, divide and store data to overcome the restrictions of physical hardware (Vennam, n.d.) are required for the cloud to work efficiently.
